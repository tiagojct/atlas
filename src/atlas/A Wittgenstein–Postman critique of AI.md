Wittgenstein and Postman seem to face the world from opposite sides: one probes the logical structure of language and its "language games"; the other warns how technology reshapes culture and public discourse. Read together, they form a sharp lens for criticising AI.

The join is this: Wittgenstein gives the tools to analyse **how** the language of AI works; Postman gives the lens to ask **what** that language does to a society.

## Three axes

### A. AI as a language game with no form of life, feeding technopoly

AI can manipulate symbols and produce coherent text, yet it understands nothing, because it inhabits no "[[Wittgenstein — language games and forms of life|form of life]]". Its mass deployment, however, builds a [[Postman — Technopoly|technopolic]] environment in which this simulation of language is treated as functionally equivalent to human thought. In the process, the genuinely human language games — the ones that demand responsibility, intention and ethical commitment — are quietly devalued.

### B. "Idiocracy" as the corrosion of language games

Postman's risk of "idiocracy" reads as a radical impoverishment of our language games. When we delegate writing, conversation and decision-making to AI for convenience, we stop practising the complex discursive skills that, for Wittgenstein, *constitute thought itself*. The "limit of my language" becomes the limit of the model that assists me.

### C. From Wittgenstein's silence to Postman's warning

The deepest questions of human life — ethics, purpose, value — belong, for the [[Wittgenstein — the Tractatus and the picture theory|Wittgenstein of the Tractatus]], to the realm of the **unsayable**. Technopoly's temptation is to formalise and compute exactly these, treating them as technical problems to optimise. AI can simulate ethical advice, but it **cannot fall silent** before what it does not know — and that inability to be silent is a source of moral danger.

## A reading path

1. **Wittgenstein, essentials:** the *Tractatus* (the closing propositions, 6.4 onward) and selected parts of the *Philosophical Investigations* (the opening, on language games).
2. **Postman, essentials:** *Amusing Ourselves to Death* and *Technopoly*.
3. **A question to sit with:** if "the limits of my language mean the limits of my world" (*Tractatus*, 5.6), how is an AI that expands my capacity to produce text while shrinking my practice of deep reflection redrawing the borders of my world?
