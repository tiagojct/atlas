Katherine Hayles, in *How We Became Posthuman: Virtual Bodies in Cybernetics, Literature, and Informatics* (1999), narrates how information "lost its body".

## The loss of the body

Hayles shows how, over the history of cybernetics and informatics, information came to be conceived as an entity separate from its material substrate. Information became something that could, in theory, migrate freely between substrates — an idea that runs through contemporary digital culture.

## Embodiment against disembodiment

Becoming posthuman can be **liberating** if it acknowledges materiality and embodiment. But it is a **nightmare** if it is the disembodiment fantasised by transhumanism — the idea of uploading the mind onto a digital substrate, which perpetuates the old Cartesian dualism and the idealist, disembodied humanist tradition.

## From cyborg to cognisphere

Hayles later came to see Haraway's very figure of the cyborg as dated. She proposed the notion of the **cognisphere** to capture the distributed networks of cognition that no longer fit the metaphor of the individual organism-machine.
