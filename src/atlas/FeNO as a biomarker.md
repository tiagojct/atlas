Fractional exhaled nitric oxide — a non-invasive biomarker of type-2 airway inflammation, used mainly in asthma management.

## What it measures

FeNO reflects eosinophilic airway inflammation. High levels point to Th2 inflammation, which responds to corticosteroids. Low levels argue against eosinophilic asthma and may suggest an alternative diagnosis.

## Clinical utility

- **Diagnosis:** it supports — it does not replace — clinical assessment.
- **Treatment monitoring:** a rising FeNO can foreshadow loss of asthma control before symptoms appear.
- **Adherence check:** persistently high FeNO despite prescribed ICS suggests poor adherence, not poor response.

## Limits

FeNO is sensitive but not very specific. Obesity, smoking, and atopic status all shift the levels. Interpretation needs clinical context — the biomarker is a clue, not a verdict.
