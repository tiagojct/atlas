Heidegger's name for the essence of modern technology — the underlying mode of revealing that makes the world show up as standing-reserve.

## What it is

Enframing is not a machine, a device, or an industry. It is a whole way of unconcealing reality: everything appears as something to be ordered, optimised, controlled, used.

## What it is not

Enframing is not a tool, not a policy, not an ideology you can simply reject. It is the *water we swim in* — a background condition of modern existence that shapes what we notice and what seems obvious.

## How it operates

- A river appears as hydroelectric potential.
- A forest appears as timber stock.
- Time appears as productivity.
- Attention appears as monetisable engagement.
- The body appears as biometric data.
- Education appears as the production of credentials.

## The existential danger

Enframing can become so dominant that we forget other ways of meeting reality — as beautiful, sacred, mysterious, or meaningful in itself. The danger is not that enframing is *false* (many of its descriptions are useful) but that it becomes the *only* way things show up.

## Freedom

For Heidegger, freedom is not to reject technology. It is to develop a relationship with technology in which enframing is recognised *as* a mode of revealing — and not mistaken for reality itself.
