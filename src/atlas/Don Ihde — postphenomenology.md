Don Ihde takes Heidegger in a more concrete, empirically grounded direction. His approach is called **postphenomenology**: the study of how specific technologies mediate human experience.

## Key moves

1. **Not "Technology" in general.** Ihde studies specific human–technology relations: glasses, microscopes, cameras, GPS, smartphones, thermometers.
2. **Mediation.** Technologies stand between humans and the world, transforming perception, embodiment, and action.
3. **Multistability.** The same technology can carry different meanings and uses in different contexts. A camera can be an artistic tool, a scientific instrument, a surveillance device, a tourist object, a memory aid.

## Human–technology relations

Ihde categorises the relations: embodiment (glasses become part of bodily perception), hermeneutic (reading a thermometer interprets the world), alterity (interacting with a robot as a quasi-other), and background (heating systems operating unnoticed).
