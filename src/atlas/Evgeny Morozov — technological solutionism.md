In *To Save Everything, Click Here* (2013), Evgeny Morozov coins and criticises the concept of **technological solutionism** — the dominant ideology in Silicon Valley that reduces every problem to a question of technical optimisation.

## Solutionism defined

Solutionism assumes that all social and political problems are:

1. **Well-defined** problems (like an engineering puzzle);
2. With **computable technical solutions**;
3. That dispense with political deliberation and conflict.

Questions of justice, distribution, values, and power are thus treated as questions of efficiency, of bugs to fix, or of interfaces to optimise.

## Depoliticisation

The central operation of solutionism is **depoliticisation**: by presenting political problems as if they were technical ones, it removes the sphere of public debate, democratic conflict, and choice between alternative futures. What is intrinsically political is treated as though it could be solved by an app, an algorithm, or a sensor.

## Critique of Zuboff

Morozov is also one of the most incisive critics of Shoshana Zuboff. In the review "Capitalism's New Clothes" (*The Baffler*, 2019), he argues that Zuboff's diagnosis isolates surveillance as a pathological deviation from an otherwise benign capitalism. For Morozov, data extraction and surveillance are not an anomaly — they are the contemporary form of capitalist exploitation, and demand a systemic critique, not a moral condemnation.
