A physiological test that measures how much air a person can breathe in and out, and how fast. The most fundamental test in respiratory medicine — and one of the most often performed badly.

## Core parameters

- **FVC:** Forced Vital Capacity — the total volume exhaled.
- **FEV₁:** Forced Expiratory Volume in the first second.
- **FEV₁/FVC ratio:** separates obstructive from restrictive patterns.

## The craft behind the numbers

Spirometry is effort-dependent. A poor manoeuvre produces misleading numbers. The technician's skill — coaching the patient, recognising submaximal effort, securing reproducibility — matters as much as the equipment.

## Interpretation

Pattern recognition: obstruction (low FEV₁/FVC), restriction (low FVC, preserved ratio), mixed patterns. Reversibility testing with a bronchodilator distinguishes asthma from COPD.
