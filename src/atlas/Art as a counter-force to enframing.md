For Heidegger, art matters deeply because it can reveal reality otherwise than technology does — not as standing-reserve, but as meaningful, mysterious, and inexhaustible.

## Two modes of revealing

- **Technology** (enframing) reveals the world as resource, stock, something to be ordered and optimised.
- **Art** (poiesis) reveals the world as meaningful, beautiful, tragic, sacred, strange — irreducible to usefulness.

## The painting of the shoes

Heidegger's famous example: Van Gogh's painting of a peasant's shoes does not make the shoes *useful*. But it reveals labour, poverty, the earth, wear, a human life and its world — dimensions invisible to technological revealing.

## Art in the technological age

Art is not an escape from technology but a counter-force from within technological society. It keeps alive the possibility of meeting things as more than resources. A poem does not optimise language — it opens language. A building can reveal dwelling, not just shelter.

## The saving power

Heidegger's enigmatic line — "where the danger grows, so grows what saves" — finds one of its meanings here: recognising that technological revealing is *a* mode, not *the* mode, opens room for art, contemplation, and non-instrumental attention.
