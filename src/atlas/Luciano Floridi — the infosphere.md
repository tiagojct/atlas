Luciano Floridi argues that digital technology transforms not only what we do, but the environment in which human existence unfolds — the **infosphere**.

## The infosphere

We no longer merely *use* digital technologies. We increasingly *inhabit* an informational environment made of data flows, digital agents, platforms, sensors, networks, and databases. The line between online and offline dissolves — we are always-already in the infosphere.

## The fourth revolution

After Copernicus (humans are not the cosmic centre), Darwin (humans are not biologically unique), and Freud (humans are not transparent to themselves), digital technology brings a fourth revolution: we begin to understand ourselves as **informational organisms** among other informational agents.

## Information environmentalism

Floridi proposes a new ethical frame: not only caring for the natural environment, but caring for the informational one — resisting information pollution, protecting informational privacy, designing for informational dignity.
