Neil Postman's central claim in *Amusing Ourselves to Death* (1985): every medium favours a certain kind of discourse, and in doing so redefines what counts as knowledge.

## Print and television

- **Print** cultivated logical, linear, conceptual thought. It demanded sustained attention and structured argument.
- **Television** turned everything into fragmented, decontextualised entertainment. Form — the moving image, the fast cut, the appeal to feeling — came to dictate content.

Postman's point is not that television *lies*, but that its very form makes certain kinds of thinking impossible.

## For AI

Swap "television" for "social feed" or "chatbot". What is the epistemology of AI? It is one of **statistical likelihood**. AI does not seek truth as correspondence to a fact (the [[Wittgenstein — the Tractatus and the picture theory|Wittgenstein of the Tractatus]]); it generates the most probable next token — a posture that can normalise constant "hallucination" and erode the very notion of truth.
