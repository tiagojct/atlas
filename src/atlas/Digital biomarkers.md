Physiological or behavioural measures collected continuously and passively through digital devices — smartphones, wearables, ambient sensors.

Unlike traditional biomarkers, measured at discrete clinic visits, digital biomarkers capture the **trajectory**: sleep patterns over weeks, changes in gait over months, typing speed as a cognitive marker.

## Key properties

- **Continuous:** not a single lab value, but a time series.
- **Ecological:** measured in the patient's environment, not the clinic.
- **Algorithmic:** requires signal processing and often ML to extract meaning.

## Examples

- Heart-rate variability from a smartwatch.
- Keystroke dynamics as a cognitive assessment.
- Step-count asymmetry after orthopaedic surgery.
- [[FeNO as a biomarker|FeNO]] would be a candidate if portable sensors became reliable.
