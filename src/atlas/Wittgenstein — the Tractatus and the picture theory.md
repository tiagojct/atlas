The early Wittgenstein, crystallised in the *Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus* (1921).

## The core idea

**Language works as a picture — a model — of reality.** A meaningful proposition is a logical representation of a possible fact in the world; the structure of the proposition mirrors the structure of the fact. This is the **picture theory** of language.

## What it implies for AI

Systems built on formal logic and symbolic representation — classic "symbolic AI" — echo this view. Knowledge is modelled as propositions that represent facts, and reasoning is the manipulation of those propositions.

## The limit

The closing line of the *Tractatus* — "Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent" (*Wovon man nicht sprechen kann, darüber muss man schweigen*) — draws a sharp ethical and epistemic boundary. For Wittgenstein, questions of value, ethics and aesthetics **fall outside the domain of meaningful discourse**.

That silence is central to a [[A Wittgenstein–Postman critique of AI|critique of AI]]: the temptation of technopoly is to formalise and compute precisely what belongs to the realm of the unsayable.
