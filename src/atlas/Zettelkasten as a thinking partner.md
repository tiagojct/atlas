A Zettelkasten (German: "slip box") is not a storage system — it is a conversation partner built from atomic, interlinked notes.

## Luhmann's insight

Niklas Luhmann, the sociologist who popularised the method, described his Zettelkasten as a *Kommunikationspartner* — a communication partner. The surprise of discovering an unexpected link between two notes is the method working. You are not retrieving information; you are being shown a connection you had not seen.

## Atomicity

Each note holds one idea, clearly stated. The unit of thought is the note, not the folder. Notes gain value through links, not through length.

## Maturity stages

- **Seedling:** a first draft, a starting point. Needs cultivation.
- **Budding:** developing, gaining links and context.
- **Evergreen:** a mature, well-linked note that has survived revision.
