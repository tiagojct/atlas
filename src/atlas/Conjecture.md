A proposition suspected to be true, but **not yet proved**.

A conjecture is a [[Theorem|theorem]] in waiting. The only difference between the two is the state of the proof: Goldbach's conjecture will be a theorem if anyone proves it, and remains a conjecture as long as no one does.

## Examples

- **Goldbach's conjecture:** "Every even number greater than 2 is the sum of two primes." Verified computationally up to 4×10¹⁸, but with no general proof.
- **Collatz conjecture:** A simple rule about sequences of integers. Verified up to 2⁶⁸, with no proof.
- **Fermat's Last Theorem:** A conjecture for 358 years, until Andrew Wiles proved it in 1994.
