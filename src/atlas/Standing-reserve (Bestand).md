Heidegger's term for how beings appear under modern technological revealing: as resources waiting to be ordered, stored, and used.

## Examples

- A forest appears as timber stock.
- A river appears as hydroelectric power.
- A field appears as agricultural yield.
- The atmosphere appears as carbon capacity.
- The human body appears as biological data.
- Time appears as units of productivity.
- Attention appears as monetisable engagement.
- Students appear as performance metrics.
- Workers appear as "human resources".

## The cruel irony

Human beings themselves become standing-reserve. We are not only the users of technological systems but also beings ordered by them. The patient becomes a medical record. The citizen becomes a data profile. The artist becomes a content producer.

## Why this matters existentially

When everything appears as standing-reserve, we lose the capacity to meet things as meaningful *in themselves*. A tree is not only biomass. A river is not only energy. A person is not only data.
