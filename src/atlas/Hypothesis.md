A term with two distinct meanings depending on context.

## Scientific context

A provisional explanation subject to empirical test. A scientific hypothesis must be **falsifiable** — it must be possible to conceive of an observation that would contradict it. "All swans are white" is a hypothesis because the observation of a black swan would falsify it.

## Mathematical context

A premise assumed within a specific argument: "let *n* be a prime number, then…". The hypothesis is not asserted as true in absolute terms; it is merely assumed for the sake of the reasoning. In this sense it approaches the [[Axiom|axiom]], but with a local and provisional scope.

## A crucial distinction

A [[Conjecture|conjecture]] aspires to be true; a scientific hypothesis aspires to be tested. The conjecture wants a proof; the hypothesis wants a test.
