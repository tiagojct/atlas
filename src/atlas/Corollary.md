A direct consequence of a [[Theorem|theorem]]. The proof of a corollary is typically very short because the heavy lifting has already been done by the theorem that precedes it.

The word comes from the Latin *corollarium*, "a small garland" — a crown offered as a bonus. The corollary is the bonus that accompanies the theorem: it demands no substantial effort, yet it is a genuine result.

## Logical structure

If *A* implies *B* (a theorem), and *B* implies *C* by a trivial step, then *C* is a corollary of *A*.
