Martin Heidegger's 1954 essay *Die Frage nach der Technik* (*The Question Concerning Technology*) is the founding text of the existential philosophy of technology.

## The central claim

> Technology's deepest problem is not that machines break down or pollute. It is that modern technology changes **the way reality shows up for us**.

Technology is not merely something humans use. It is a way of *revealing* the world.

## The key moves

1. **The instrumental view is correct but shallow.** Technology really is a means to an end and a human activity — but that misses the deeper question.
2. **Technology reveals.** As a microscope reveals a cellular world, modern technology reveals the world under a particular interpretation: as resource, as stock, as something to be ordered up.
3. **Ancient vs modern.** Older craft (*poiesis*, a bringing-forth) worked *with* its materials. Modern technology *challenges* nature to deliver resources on demand.
4. **Standing-reserve (*Bestand*).** Every being — nature, people, time, attention — appears as a resource waiting to be ordered and optimised.
5. **Enframing (*Gestell*).** The underlying mode of revealing that makes the world appear as standing-reserve. Not a machine, but a whole way of unconcealing reality.
6. **The danger is existential, not physical.** Enframing can grow so dominant that we forget other ways of meeting reality: as beautiful, sacred, mysterious, meaningful in itself.
7. **The saving power.** Where the danger grows, so does what saves. To recognise enframing *as* enframing opens the possibility of a freer relationship with technology.
