The ability to read, interpret, criticise, and communicate health data — a prerequisite for evidence-based practice in the 21st century.

## Why it matters

Health professionals are trained to read clinical guidelines, not to interrogate the data that produced them. When AI begins to generate those guidelines — or to claim it does — the ability to assess the data pipeline becomes a clinical skill.

## Components

- Understanding data provenance: where did this number come from?
- Recognising patterns of omission: who is *not* in this dataset?
- Interpreting uncertainty: what is the interval, not just the point estimate?
- Spotting confounding by indication in observational data.

## The educational gap

Most health curricula teach statistics as a standalone module. Health data literacy requires integrating data reasoning into every clinical discipline — not as a separate island.
