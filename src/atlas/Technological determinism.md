The idea that technology develops according to its own logic and then forces society to adapt.

## The strong version

> Once a technology exists, society will inevitably reorganise itself around it.

The usual examples: print transforming religion and education, the factory transforming work, the internet transforming communication, AI transforming knowledge work.

## The critique

Technologies do not arise from nowhere. They are shaped by politics, economics, culture, military needs, regulation, design decisions, and social values. The internet could have developed in more public, decentralised, commercial, or surveillance-oriented directions. Its current form was not the only one possible.

## A balanced position

Society shapes technology, and technology then reshapes society. Neither pure determinism nor pure voluntarism captures the whole picture.
