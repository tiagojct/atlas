In the strict sense, a proposition that is defended — asserted, with a readiness to argue in its favour.

In academic settings it took on the meaning of a research work (a master's or doctoral dissertation), which has somewhat obscured the original sense. But the root remains: a doctoral thesis is, literally, a proposition the candidate defends before a committee.

## Origin

From the Greek *thésis*, "an act of placing, a position". A thesis is a position one occupies and defends.

## Distinction

Unlike the [[Axiom|axiom]] (which is accepted) and the [[Theorem|theorem]] (which is proved), a thesis is argued. Its domain is not formal logic, but rhetoric and academic argumentation.
