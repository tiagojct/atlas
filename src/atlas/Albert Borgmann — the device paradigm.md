Albert Borgmann's *Technology and the Character of Contemporary Life* (1984) brings Heidegger down into modern everyday life through the concept of the **device paradigm**.

## The device paradigm

A device delivers a commodity (warmth, music, food, information) while hiding the machinery, labour, skill, and context behind it.

Central heating gives you warmth instantly — but compare it with a fireplace: gathering wood, tending the flame, bodily presence, family gathering, rhythm, attention.

## The pattern

Devices separate the commodity from the context that once gave it meaning. The result is disengagement: we consume more, but engage less.

## Focal things and practices

Borgmann contrasts devices with [[Focal practices|focal practices]] — activities that gather attention, skill, body, world, and community: cooking a meal, playing music, walking, gardening, making art, repairing something, gathering around a table.

## The question

Does technology free us for deeper engagement, or does it replace engagement with effortless consumption?
