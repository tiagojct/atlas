Peter-Paul Verbeek extends postphenomenology into ethics: technologies do not merely mediate perception — they mediate **morality**.

## The central claim

> Technologies help shape what people do and how they decide. They take part in moral situations.

## Examples

- A speed bump encourages slower driving — it *does* ethics.
- A fitness app encourages self-monitoring — it shapes values about health and the body.
- An ultrasound shapes decisions about pregnancy — it mediates moral choices about life.
- Social platforms shape recognition, comparison, and outrage — they mediate social morality.
- Recommendation algorithms shape taste and attention — they mediate desire.

## The ethics of design

Verbeek argues that ethics should not arrive only *after* design, as a separate assessment. Ethics should be built *into* design. Designers are doing ethics, whether they acknowledge it or not.
