For Heidegger, technology is not merely a tool — it is a way of *revealing* (*Entbergen*) the world. Different modes of technology unconceal reality under different interpretations.

## Bringing-forth (poiesis)

The Greek word *poiesis* — the root of "poetry" — means letting something emerge into presence. A flower opening is a bringing-forth. A poem being written is a bringing-forth. Traditional craft — a carpenter working with the grain, the give, and the texture of the wood — is also a bringing-forth.

## Challenging-forth

Modern technology does not bring things into presence gently. It *challenges* nature to deliver resources. The hydroelectric dam does not reveal the river as a river — it reveals it as energy potential. This is not bringing-forth, but extracting, ordering, storing.

## The contrast

| Bringing-forth (poiesis) | Challenging-forth |
|---|---|
| Works with the qualities of the material | Extracts from the material |
| Respects limits and resistances | Overrides limits |
| The thing appears as itself | The thing appears as a resource |
| Craft, art, poetry | Industrial extraction, data mining |
