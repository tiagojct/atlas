Jacques Ellul's central concept is *la technique*: the total system of methods, procedures, calculations, and organisational practices oriented toward maximum efficiency.

## What "technique" is

Technique is not just machines. It is the spread of technical rationality across every domain: education, war, medicine, administration, politics, communication, urban planning, work, leisure.

## The imperative of efficiency

Modern society is increasingly governed by a single question: *what is the most efficient method?* This logic propagates without anyone explicitly choosing it. We stop asking "what is good?" and start asking "what works best?"

## The autonomy of technique

Ellul argues that technique becomes autonomous — a self-perpetuating system that follows its own logic of efficiency, beyond human control. We may feel we choose our technologies, but technique increasingly chooses for us.

## The Heideggerian link

Heidegger's [[Enframing (Gestell)|enframing (Gestell)]] is the metaphysical diagnosis; Ellul's technique is the sociological manifestation. Both describe a world where everything appears through the lens of optimisation and control.
