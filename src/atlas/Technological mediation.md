The idea — central to postphenomenology — that technologies stand between humans and the world, transforming how we perceive, act, and understand.

## What mediation means

A pair of glasses is not just a "tool for seeing". It transforms the very act of seeing. After years of wearing glasses, the world-without-glasses is no longer what it was. The technology mediated — not merely assisted — perception.

## Dimensions of mediation

- **Perceptual:** what becomes visible? What becomes harder to notice?
- **Bodily:** how does the body's relation to space, effort, and skill change?
- **Action:** what new actions become possible? What old ones become obsolete?
- **Hermeneutic:** how does the technology interpret the world before we do? (A thermometer translates bodily sensation into numbers.)

## The GPS example

Before GPS, navigation demanded attention to landmarks, direction, memory, and local orientation. With GPS, you arrive more efficiently but know the place less intimately. The question is not "did GPS help?" but "what kind of spatial being am I becoming?"
