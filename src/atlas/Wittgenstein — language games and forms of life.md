The later Wittgenstein, set out in the *Philosophical Investigations* (published posthumously, 1953). This is the richer phase for a critique of AI.

## The core idea

Wittgenstein turns against his earlier self. The meaning of a word **is not fixed**; it lives in its **use** within a social and practical context. Language is a multiplicity of "language games" (*Sprachspiele*), each woven into a "form of life" (*Lebensform*) — shared social, cultural and bodily practices.

## What counts as a language game

Giving orders, describing an object, telling a joke, apologising, praying, making a promise. Each has its own rules and only makes sense inside the form of life that sustains it.

## What it implies for AI

Large Language Models are trained to recognise and reproduce statistical patterns in how language is used. But on a Wittgensteinian reading, **AI does not genuinely play the language game**, because it has no form of life — none of the embodied experience, intention or cultural context that gives words their meaning.

AI manipulates symbols; it does not inhabit the world that makes those symbols mean anything.
