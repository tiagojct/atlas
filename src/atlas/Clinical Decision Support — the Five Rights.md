Osheroff's framework for effective clinical decision support: the right **information**, to the right **person**, in the right **format**, through the right **channel**, at the right **time** in the workflow.

## The five rights

1. **Right information** — evidence-based, actionable, not generic advice.
2. **Right person** — the clinician making the decision, not the department head.
3. **Right format** — one line in the EHR, not a 14-page PDF.
4. **Right channel** — inside the tool the clinician is already using, not a separate portal.
5. **Right time** — at the moment of decision, not during the morning meeting.

## Why most CDSS fail

They hit one or two rights and miss the rest. A perfectly accurate alert delivered through a pop-up that fires twenty times a day is not a right-channel or right-time intervention — it is noise.
