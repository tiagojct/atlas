Historically used as a synonym for [[Axiom|axiom]], but with a nuance: postulates were propositions that one was *asked* to accept — hard to prove, yet still plausible.

The word comes from the Latin *postulare*, "to ask, to demand". A postulate was less "this is self-evident" (axiom) and more "grant me this so we can proceed".

Today the distinction between axiom and postulate is all but erased. In contemporary mathematical practice they are treated as equivalent. The difference survives mainly in texts on the history and philosophy of mathematics.
