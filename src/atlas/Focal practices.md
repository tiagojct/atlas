Albert Borgmann's term for activities that gather attention, skill, body, world, and community — as opposed to devices that deliver commodities while hiding the context.

## Examples

- Cooking a meal (vs meal delivery)
- Playing a musical instrument (vs streaming)
- Walking or running outdoors (vs treadmill metrics)
- Gardening (vs supermarket delivery)
- Making art or craft (vs consuming content)
- Repairing something (vs replacing it)
- Gathering around a table (vs eating alone with screens)
- Writing by hand, or with care (vs AI-generated text)

## What makes a practice focal

A focal practice is not just a hobby. It is an activity that centres attention, engages the body, ties into a tradition or skill, and discloses a world. It resists the split between commodity and context that defines the [[Albert Borgmann — the device paradigm|device paradigm]].

## The existential dimension

Focal practices are not about rejecting technology. They are about preserving ways of being that technology tends to dissolve. They answer the question: *what kind of engagement makes a life meaningful?*
