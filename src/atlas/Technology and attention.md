One of the most important existential themes in the philosophy of technology: attention is not just a mental resource — it is the way the world becomes meaningful to you.

## Why attention matters existentially

What you repeatedly attend to shapes your desires, emotions, sense of importance, memory, identity, and moral imagination. When technologies compete for and capture attention, they are not merely "wasting time" — they are shaping the person.

## What attention-driven technology produces

A platform designed for continuous engagement trains the self toward: interruption, comparison, speed, novelty, outrage, performance, impatience, shallow recognition.

## The question of freedom

> If my attention is constantly steered by technical systems, in what sense are my desires still mine?

Freedom is no longer just "I can choose". It becomes: *who or what is shaping the field within which I choose?*

## The infrastructure of attention

Attention is not only individual — it is infrastructural. The design of cities, classrooms, workplaces, and digital platforms configures what we can attend to, and for how long. [[Albert Borgmann — the device paradigm|Borgmann's]] focal practices are precisely activities that gather and sustain attention against dispersal.
