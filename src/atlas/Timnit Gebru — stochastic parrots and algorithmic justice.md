Timnit Gebru is the central figure of the algorithmic-justice and present-harm agenda, in contrast to the existential-risk agenda.

## Gender Shades (2018)

With Joy Buolamwini, she showed empirically that commercial gender-classification systems failed in starkly unequal ways: darker-skinned women had error rates up to 34.7%, against just 0.8% for lighter-skinned men — framing **algorithmic bias** as a question of justice, not mere technical accuracy.

## Stochastic Parrots (2021)

The paper *On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots*, with Emily Bender, Angelina McMillan-Major, and Margaret Mitchell, argues that large language models are "stochastic parrots": systems that statistically recombine linguistic forms without understanding or intention. It names three dangers: high environmental costs (a single model can emit ~284 tonnes of CO₂), bias amplification, and opacity. The controversy around the paper led to Gebru's forced exit from Google.

## DAIR and the critique of existential risk

Gebru founded the **Distributed AI Research Institute** (DAIR). Her position is explicitly critical of the "existential risk" agenda: it serves to divert attention from concrete harms — labour exploitation, racial discrimination — and to concentrate authority in the very builders of these systems.
