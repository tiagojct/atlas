Donna Haraway's *Cyborg Manifesto* (1985) challenges the boundary between human and machine, nature and culture.

## The cyborg

The cyborg — part organism, part machine — is not a futurist fantasy but a description of contemporary human existence. We are always entangled with animals, tools, machines, environments, histories, bodies, and social systems. Humans were never pure, isolated, self-sufficient beings.

## What the cyborg unsettles

Traditional boundaries dissolve: human/machine, nature/culture, male/female, organism/technology, natural/artificial. These boundaries were never innocent — they served to exclude and to rank.

## The political promise

Where Heidegger sometimes suggests a loss of some more original relation to being, Haraway embraces hybridity as a site of possibility. The cyborg can disrupt oppressive ideas of purity, nature, and identity. It opens new forms of kinship, responsibility, and politics — not despite technology, but through it.
