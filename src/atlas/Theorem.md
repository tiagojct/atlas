A proposition demonstrated from [[Axiom|axioms]] (and from other already-proved theorems), using rules of inference.

The **Pythagorean theorem** is not assumed — it is proved. The demonstration is precisely what distinguishes a theorem from an [[Axiom|axiom]]: the axiom is the starting point, the theorem is the destination of valid reasoning.

A theorem can, in turn, serve as the basis for proving further theorems. The deductive chain — axioms → theorems → more theorems — is the backbone of formal systems.

## Close relatives

- [[Lemma|Lemma]]: also proved, but auxiliary.
- [[Corollary|Corollary]]: also proved, but a direct consequence of another theorem.
- [[Conjecture|Conjecture]]: a theorem that has not yet been proved.
