An auxiliary [[Theorem|theorem]]. It is formally demonstrated, but its interest is instrumental: it serves to prove another, more important result.

A lemma is rarely cited for its own sake; it is a connecting piece within a larger demonstration. The word comes from the Greek *lêmma*, "something received or taken" — the lemma is taken as a step on the way to the main theorem.

## Example

**Zorn's Lemma** is an important result in set theory, but its name as a "lemma" reflects the role it originally played: Zorn proved it in order to use it in the demonstration of another result.
