The Methods section is where a paper lives or dies. The Results may be brilliant; the Discussion may be eloquent. But if the Methods do not hold up, nothing else matters.

## Why methods are undervalued

- Readers skip them; reviewers who know the field read them first.
- Journals push methods to the end or into supplementary material — a design choice that signals their perceived priority.
- Methods are hard to write: they demand precision, honesty about limitations, and resistance to flourish.

## What a good Methods section does

- Makes the study **reproducible**: someone else could do the same.
- Reveals the **decisions**: why this design, this population, this analysis?
- Acknowledges the **trade-offs**: what was sacrificed, and why.

## The pedagogical corollary

Students are taught to write Introduction → Methods → Results → Discussion. They should be taught that the Methods section is the centre of gravity — the part you cannot fake.
