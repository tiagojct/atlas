A digital garden is a public, non-chronological collection of evolving notes — a middle point between a private notebook and a polished publication.

## Gardens vs blogs

- **Blogs** are chronological streams. Posts have dates; old posts sink.
- **Gardens** are spatial networks. Notes are linked by concept, not by time. Old notes are tended, not archived.

## Key properties

- **Imperfect by design:** notes can be seedlings. Not everything needs to be finished.
- **Bidirectional links:** each link creates a visible backlink. Structure emerges from connections.
- **Evergreen notes:** notes that are revisited and rewritten, not published and abandoned.

## The academic garden

A digital garden is a natural companion to research: it makes thinking visible, invites collaboration, and lowers the barrier between "having an idea" and "sharing that idea".
