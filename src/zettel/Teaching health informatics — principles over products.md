Teaching health informatics should teach principles — not this month's EHR interface. A student who understands data modelling, workflow analysis, and evaluation methods can adapt to any tool. A student trained on Epic will be obsolete when the contract changes.

## Core principles

- **Abstraction:** what clinical concept lies behind the form field?
- **Workflow:** where does the information go, and who acts on it?
- **Evaluation:** how do you know the system is helping?
- **Interoperability:** why don't these two systems talk, and what would it take?

## The product trap

Teaching specific software produces graduates who can operate tools but cannot design or evaluate them. The shelf life of product knowledge is measured in versions; the shelf life of principles is measured in careers.

## The practical test

After the course, can the student look at a new health-IT system and ask the right questions — not "how do I click this button?" but "what assumptions are built into this workflow?"
