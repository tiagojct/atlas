In *Technopoly* (1992), Postman describes a society in which technology stops being a tool and becomes the cultural paradigm itself — dictating values, priorities, and the very definition of knowledge.

## Three key ideas

### Idiocracy

The triumph of convenience over critical thought. We hand decisions to automatic systems and let our intellectual autonomy waste away. It is the "Google effect" raised to its highest power.

### Dehumanisation

The steady replacement of genuine human relationships with machine-mediated interaction, eroding empathy and social bonds.

### The sovereignty of the technical expert

Knowledge fragments, and only the experts who command the technical language hold authority — undermining public morality and common sense.

## For AI

Generative AI is the contemporary face of technopoly: it promises convenience and productivity, but the price may be the atrophy of the very capacities that define us as beings who think, deliberate, and take responsibility.
