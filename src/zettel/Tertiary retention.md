Bernard Stiegler's concept for memory stored outside the living body — writing, photography, cinema, archives, databases, and digital media.

## The three retentions

1. **Primary retention:** immediate perception — hearing a melody as it unfolds, moment to moment.
2. **Secondary retention:** personal, lived memory — what you can recall of your own past.
3. **Tertiary retention:** memory externalised in technical objects — a photograph, a book, a recording, a database.

## Why it matters

Tertiary retention is not just a convenient storage medium. It reorganises how humans remember, imagine, desire, and transmit culture. Writing changed oral memory. Photography changed visual memory. Digital storage is changing all forms of memory at once.

## The contemporary condition

Today, memory is increasingly externalised into photo libraries, cloud storage, search histories, chats, calendar reminders, social timelines, and recommendation feeds. This grants extraordinary powers of preservation — but it can also turn recollection from inner digestion into data retrieval. A remembered event becomes less something *digested* and more something *retrieved*.
