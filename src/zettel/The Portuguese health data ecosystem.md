The health-data landscape in Portugal — a national health service (SNS) with centralised infrastructure, regional variation, and an evolving digital strategy.

## The main actors

- **SPMS:** Serviços Partilhados do Ministério da Saúde — the central IT authority of the SNS.
- **SNS 24:** the citizen-facing digital portal and app.
- **PEM:** Processo Eletrónico de Saúde — the national EHR summary, accessible across institutions.
- **RSE:** Registo de Saúde Eletrónico — the platform for sharing clinical data between providers.

## The current situation

Portugal has national infrastructure many countries envy — a single-payer system, centralised procurement, and a growing digital-health strategy. Yet interoperability between hospital systems remains uneven, data quality for research is variable, and the regulatory framework for secondary use of health data is still evolving (the European Health Data Space implementation is pending).

## The research opportunity

The combination of a universal health system, longitudinal data, and a relatively stable population makes Portugal an attractive setting for real-world-evidence studies — if the data-access pathways can be made efficient and transparent.
