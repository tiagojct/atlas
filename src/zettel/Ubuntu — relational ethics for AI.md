Ubuntu, the relational philosophy of sub-Saharan and Southern Africa, offers one of the liveliest contributions to the ethics of AI today.

## Relational personhood

The maxim *umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu* — "a person is a person through other persons" — defines personhood not as a biological given or a property of the rational individual, but as something one **becomes** through relations of reciprocity and care within a community. One is not born a person: one becomes a person in relation.

## Application to AI (Mhlambi, 2020)

Sabelo Mhlambi, in the report *From Rationality to Relationality* (Carr Center, Harvard, 2020), argues that the harms of AI — automated decisions, biases — are in essence violations of relational personhood. He proposes shifting the foundation of AI ethics from rationality (the Cartesian and Enlightenment inheritance) to **relationality**.

## Internal tensions

Thaddeus Metz has developed a systematic relational ethics out of Ubuntu. The internal critique (from the EthicsLab at the University of Cape Town, for instance) is that Ubuntu is often invoked as a "password" without showing how it actually reorients ethical analysis.
