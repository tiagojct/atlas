One of the first web-based clinical decision-support systems for interpreting exhaled nitric oxide ([[FeNO as a biomarker|FeNO]]) measurements. Developed in the early 2010s at FMUP.

## What it did

It took a FeNO value plus clinical context (age, height, atopy, symptoms), applied evidence-based interpretation algorithms, and produced a structured report for clinicians.

## Why it matters as a research model

eNO.VIS is an example of **building tools as a research contribution** — not a paper *about* a tool, but a tool that operationalises the evidence. The tool itself is the research output, alongside its validation studies.

This is a different model from the standard academic publish-and-move-on cycle. The tool persists, is maintained, adapts to new evidence, and accumulates clinical experience.

## Lessons

- Building the tool exposes gaps in the evidence that no review article would surface.
- Clinicians adopt tools, not guidelines. A CDSS is a guideline that actually runs.
- Maintenance is the hard part — not the initial development.
