The model in which AI supports — rather than replaces — clinical decision-making. This is not a compromise position; it is the only model consistent with clinical accountability.

## Augmentation, not automation

- The clinician remains responsible for the decision.
- The AI provides information, suggestions, or checks.
- The clinician–AI interaction is the unit of evaluation, not the AI in isolation.

## Empirical evidence

- Radiologists + AI can outperform either alone — but only when the AI's confidence is well calibrated and the interface is well designed.
- When the AI is overconfident, or the interface is poorly integrated, the human+AI team can perform *worse* than the human alone.
- Clinicians who understand the model's limits collaborate better; those who treat it as an oracle do worse.
