Mobile-health apps and interventions need evaluation frameworks that go beyond clinical trials — and certainly beyond app-store ratings.

## Why standard RCTs fall short

- mHealth interventions evolve faster than trial timelines.
- The "active ingredient" is often unclear: is it the app, the notifications, the social support, or the combination?
- Context matters enormously: an app that works in a Scandinavian primary-care setting may fail in a different health system.

## Frameworks

- **WHO mHealth evidence reporting and assessment (mERA):** a checklist for reporting mHealth studies.
- **MARS:** Mobile App Rating Scale — a subjective quality assessment.
- **RE-AIM:** Reach, Effectiveness, Adoption, Implementation, Maintenance — focused on implementation.
- **NICE Evidence Standards Framework for Digital Health Technologies:** stratified by function (tier A: system impact; tier C: simple information).

## The key question

Not "does this app work?" but "for whom, in what context, through what mechanism, and compared with what alternative?"
