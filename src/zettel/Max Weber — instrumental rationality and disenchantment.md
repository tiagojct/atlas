Max Weber provides the founding sociological diagnosis of what will become the critique of technical rationality.

## Zweckrationalität vs Wertrationalität

Weber distinguishes two kinds of rationality:

- **Zweckrationalität** (instrumental, ends-oriented rationality): calculates the most efficient means to a given end, *without questioning the end itself*.
- **Wertrationalität** (value-oriented rationality): acts on ethical, religious, or aesthetic conviction, *regardless of the instrumental consequences*.

## The disenchantment of the world

Modernity is, for Weber, the process of *Entzauberung der Welt* — the disenchantment of the world: the progressive subjection of every sphere of life (economy, politics, law, art, religion) to calculation, predictability, and efficiency. Mystery, the sacred, and the incommensurable retreat before the logic of means.

## The iron cage

Rationalisation culminates in the "iron cage" (*stahlhartes Gehäuse*) of bureaucracy: an impersonal system of rules and procedures that, created to serve human ends, becomes an end in itself and imprisons those it was meant to serve.

## Legacy

This diagnosis is the ground on which the [[The Frankfurt School — dialectic of enlightenment|Frankfurt School]] builds: Horkheimer and Adorno radicalise Zweckrationalität into "instrumental reason" and show how enlightenment turns into myth. [[Jacques Ellul — technique|Ellul]], in a distinct sociological register, describes the total spread of this logic under the name of *la technique*.
