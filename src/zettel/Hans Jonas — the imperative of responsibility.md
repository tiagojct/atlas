Hans Jonas's *The Imperative of Responsibility* (1979) argues that modern technology changes the scale of human action, demanding a fundamentally new ethics.

## The change of scale

Older ethical systems assumed that human actions had a limited reach — touching neighbours, family, the polis. Modern technologies can affect the planet, future generations, ecosystems, genetic life, nuclear survival, the climate.

## The new imperative

> Act so that the consequences of your action are compatible with the permanence of genuine human life.

This is not a rule for individual conduct alone. It applies to collective, technological, and political decisions whose effects reach far into the future.

## The heuristic of fear

Jonas proposes giving more weight to worst-case forecasts than to best-case hopes. When the irreversible is at stake (species extinction, climate collapse), precaution is not paranoia — it is responsibility.
